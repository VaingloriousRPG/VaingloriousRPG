Graymore

Unlike the rest of humankind, the modest Graymore is arguably the most difficult to keep track of. Walking among the masses much like a ghost of the pale. They seem almost invisible, unless one knows they are looking for a Graymore, of course. The Graymore’s culture focuses heavily on reenforcing the need to remain undetected and not cause waves. They are never ones to make a spectacle of themselves. Instead, they would prefer to stay quiet and fade into the background.

Graymore Humans are taught to not be noticed by others. The culture believes in a level of polite privacy rarely seen. They teach the best way to show respect is to not interfere with anothers life. Therefore, it is best to be unobtrusive and let others enjoy their life. Many of the other races won’t take notice of a graymore as a common crowd passes. This does not mean those specifically looking for Graymore won’t see them. Graymore are not hiding or invisible. They merely take advantage of the local culture to not be where most are watching. They are trying to be respectful.

The Graymore have no titles or honorifics to distinguish them or their station beyond titles that are routine within most communities like ‘elder’ or ‘guard’.
