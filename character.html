Laxen (Dwarf)

Deep dwellers with vision adapted for low and infrared light. Daylight requires the use of tinted goggles. Laxen are often Rogues or Fighters, less often Casters or Hunters.

Murick (Dwarf)

Murick are surface prospectors who have adapted to life on the surface, prefering fresh air and sunlight to dank recesses. Fighter class suites them best, but they can make a fair Caster.

Dark Elf

Dark Elves eschew sunlight and open air, prefering to build their settlements underground. They take readily to the life of a Caster or a Fighter. At times, even a Rogue's life is not far off their predilections

Grove Elf

Quiet elves who find a home in the woods and curate grove villages there. Leaving little trace of their passing outdoors makes them consumate Hunters. While their attributes lend to Caster.
As a Rogue or Fighter, they are average.

High Elf

Tall regal elves to whom propriety and etiquette abide in their very beings. These elves may be natural diplomats in most any situation. Casting and Fighting are appropriate, while Hunting and Rougish activities are disdained.

Graymore (Human)

A society of Humans who value obscurity. They prefer to glide among others with little fanfare. These tendencies lend themselves to the Rogue or Caster profession. Occasionally they will be Hunters, rarely do they choose Fighter.

Halfling

A short happy folk who appreciate a good meal. Halflings often find pleasure as Rogues or Hunters. Casting can be rewarding though a Fighter's life is a bit rough.

Mountaineer (Human)

A hardy Human race characterized by booming voices. Mountaineers make excellent Fighters or Hunters and decent Rogues. Few are Casters.

Low lander (Human)

Ubiquitous humans found in most settings. Lowlanders have an eye for bargains that often nets a discount for common items. Hunting comes easily to Low Landers. Casting & Fighting are fair. Oddly, Rogue does not suit them well.

Minotaur (Oxen Folk)

Horn and shoulders above the elves, these Tenacious fighters strut a heightened sense of rightness and honor. Minotaur Fighters abound, some are Hunters, and a few are Rogues. Casters are rare.

Presma (Feline)

Cat-people who have retained the speed and cunning of their ancestors. Presma tend towards Rogue and Hunter classes, at times even becoming Fighters. Casting is less advantageous.

Sprite (Fairy)

Sprites enjoy the high life. Though small in stature, their wings lift them above the crowd, up a full 30 feet. Sprites fly into Casting and Rogue work with zeal. Hunting makes great sport, Fighting weighs them down.

Vocqua (Finfolk)

Infamous hunters and warriors of the sea, Vocqua have all but perfected the art of the trident. Voqua prefer Hunter and Fighter classes. While some are Rogues, almost none are Casters.

Piscine-Fey (Finfolk)

Fin folk, as they often refer to themselves, are an enchanting subaquatic race with both fish and elven-like features. Piscine apply well to Casting and Rogue classes. Though slight, some few are Hunters.

Tiro

Tiro is the beginning of adventuring. All adventurers start at Tier Zero. This tier may be used by your GM to introduce your character to the world or it may be skipped altogether in favor of starting right into your chosen class.

Fighter

Fighters train for close in combat. Their strength and skills focus on closing with the enemy quickly and holding or advancing against them. Fighters are trained in a wide array of weapons and armor.

Rogue

A silent specialist of misdirection and deceit. Rogue skills favor an indirect approach to most problems, even combat. Masters of back alleys and busy markets, they are most at home in civilization.

Hunter

These woodsmen are at home plying their trade deep in the forest. Foraging and hunting fills their pockets as well as their spirit.
Bows, tracking, and concoctions embody a Hunter's skill set.

Strumos

Healing casters who may partner with Fighters through Nae'Ems. These characters are skilled at healing and applying healing concoctions. Strumos are the only casters able to raise dead at higher levels.

Sylvan

Nature casters who may partner with Hunters through Nae'Ems. Their spells concentrate on interacting with nature and include offensive ice spells. Sylvan animal spells make for excellent scouting abilities.

Dokour

Battle casters who do not partner with other classes. Dokour use fire and the dark arts to defeat opponents. These universally angry casters oppose any of their kind who would pretend to be compassionate.

Eolas

Information Casters who may partner with Rogues through Nae'Ems. These scribes of the casting world often associate with Rogue guilds as their proclivities toward gathering information are complimentary.
