Welcome to Vainglorious.

Vainglorious is an old-school role-playing game with an emphasis on player interaction.
Players volunteer to join a group with the goal of bringing a unique adventure to life.
A Gamemaster organizes and creates the world through which players experience adventures with their characters and imagination.

This system is designed to support a wide range of play styles, providing a framework of rules and methods for creating and interacting with a vibrant mythical world.
Magic spells, combat, movement, concoctions, planar travel, and the passage of time are all defined and designed for ease of use and logical connection.

A wide variety of character classes allow players to experience adventures from a distinct perspective with widely varying spells, skills, and abilities.
Combining unique strengths and weaknesses, each class allows players to solve problems and complete quests through wit and guile.

The passage of time plays a significant role in all aspects of the game. Concoctions go bad, spells wane, and the world moves along.
Tracking time both during and between adventures adds a sense of realism to what otherwise might seem like a static world.
Gamemasters are encouraged to integrate the passage of time into adventures.

Movement on the world map has been simplified to a mark system.
Marks are a loosely defined distance that a humanoid can normally walk in one day.
That distance varies by terrain and roads, allowing for a more organic feel than grid systems.

We hope that you will find these rules as useful, exciting, and entertaining as we do.

Dwarf Laxen

Deep dwellers with vision adapted for low and infrared light. Daylight requires the use of tinted goggles. Laxen are often Rogues or Fighters, less often Casters or Hunters.

Dwarf Murick

Murick are surface prospectors who have adapted to life on the surface, prefering fresh air and sunlight to dank recesses. Fighter class suites them best, but they can make a fair Caster.

Dark Elf

Dark Elves eschew sunlight and open air, prefering to build their settlements underground. They take readily to the life of a Caster or a Fighter. A Rogue's life is also not far off their predilections

High Elf

Graymore

A society of Humans who value obscurity. They prefer to glide among others with little fanfare. These tendencies lend themselves to the Rogue or Caster profession. Occasionally they will be Hunters, rarely do they choose Fighter.

Halfling

A short happy folk who appreciate a good meal. Halflings often find pleasure as Rogues or Hunters. Casting can be rewarding though a Fighter's life is a bit rough.

Mountaineer

A hardy Human race characterized by booming voices. Mountaineers make excellent Fighters or Hunters and decent Rogues. Few are Casters.

Lowlander

Minotaur

Tenacious fighters with a heightened sense of rightness and a willingness to put nothing above honor. Minotaurs Fighters abound, some are Hunters, and a few are Rogues. Casters are rare.

Presma

Cat-people who have retained the speed and cunning of their ancestors. Presma tend towards Rogue and Hunter classes, at times even becoming Fighters. Casting is less advantageous.

Voldyar

Sprite

Sprites enjoy the high life. Though small in stature, their wings lift them above the crowd, tho no more than 30 feet.
Sprites fly into Casting and Rogue work with zeal. Hunting makes great sport, but Fighting weighs them down.

Vocqua

Infamous hunters and warriors of the sea, Vocqua have all but perfected the art of the trident. Voqua prefer Hunter and Fighter classes. While some are Rogues, almost none are Casters.

Piscine-Fey

Fin folk, as they often refer to themselves, are an enchanting subaquatic race with both fish and elven-like features. Piscine apply well to Casting and Rogue classes. Though slight, some few are Hunters.

Tiro

Tiro is the beginning of adventuring. All adventurers start at Tier Zero. This tier may be used by your GM to introduce your character to the world or it may be skipped altogether in favor of starting right into your chosen class.
